# FeeEdge reply playbook (@Fee_Edge)

The daily 20 to 30 replies, made fast but kept human. Use this to find targets,
pick an angle, and paste a draft you tweak in 10 seconds. Voice is the locked Edie
style: lowercase, conversational, dash free, at most one 🤓, value first, link only
when it actually fits.

The golden rule: most replies carry no link. You are building recognition and
goodwill. Drop feeedge.com only when someone is literally asking which venue is
cheapest, complaining about fees or funding, or comparing exchanges. Roughly 1 in 4
replies should carry a link, the rest are pure value.

---

## Part 1: who to reply to

### Account types to hunt
1. Mid tier perps and futures traders (5k to 100k followers). Sweet spot. Big enough
   for reach, small enough that they reply back.
2. Trading educators and "edge" accounts that post about costs, risk, and P&L.
3. Exchange and funding chatter: anyone posting screenshots of fees, funding bills,
   or "which exchange should I use" questions.
4. Quote tweet bait: big accounts asking "what exchange do you use and why".
5. Crypto data and analytics accounts (they attract a numbers minded audience).

Avoid: pump and signal accounts, giveaway accounts, anything that looks like a bot
farm. Wrong audience and bad for the brand.

### Search queries to find fresh targets fast
Paste these into X search, sort by Latest, reply to the good ones:
- "taker fees" OR "maker fees"
- "funding rate" perps
- "which exchange" cheaper OR fees
- "trading fees" eating OR killing OR bleeding
- binance OR bybit fees high
- "30 day volume" tier
- perps "funding" cost
- "best exchange for" futures OR perps

## Part 2: reply drafts by scenario

Pick the scenario that matches the post, grab a draft, swap in their detail, send.
All drafts are dash free and in voice. The (link) tag means it is okay to add the
soft link; lines without it should stay link free.

### A. Someone complains their fees are too high (link)
- yeah taker fees stack up way faster than the headline rate makes it feel. the number that actually hurt me was a full month of fills added up. worth totaling yours once, it is eye opening
- the rate looks tiny until you multiply by monthly volume. if most of your fills are market orders you are paying the worst price on the menu every time. you can plug your numbers in at feeedge.com and see the real all in
- honestly depends on your volume and whether you add or take liquidity. the cheapest venue for you might not be the one you are on. feeedge.com ranks them for your exact profile if you want to check

### B. Someone griping about funding on perps (link)
- funding is the silent one. hold through a few windows and it can cost more than the trade itself. the cheapest venue to trade is not always the cheapest to hold
- this is the bit people forget when they say a venue is "free". funding every 8 hours adds up on a leveraged hold. feeedge.com factors funding plus fees so you see the real cost of holding there
- felt this. the trade fee and the hold cost are two different questions and funding decides the second one

### C. "Which exchange should I use" / "what do you trade on" (link)
- depends way more on your volume and style than people admit. a scalper at 2m a month and someone swinging 50k want totally different venues. feeedge.com ranks all 20 for your numbers, free to check
- the real answer is "the cheapest for how you trade". run your volume and maker taker mix through feeedge.com, the ranking shifts a lot with both
- whatever people name first is usually not the cheapest for your profile. worth checking your own numbers before you commit

### D. Someone posts a fee or funding screenshot (link when natural)
- that is a real number once you annualize it. wild how invisible it feels month to month
- this is exactly the leak most traders never total. if you want the all in across venues feeedge.com does the math for your profile
- the headline rate hides this. nice that you actually added it up, most people never do

### E. "Zero fee" or "free trading" claims (link)
- zero fee is a headline not the whole bill. funding, spreads and withdrawals still cost you. add it all up before calling it cheap
- a zero fee venue with a wide spread is not actually cheap. total cost is what matters, not the sticker rate
- free on the trade fee, sure. then funding and the spread quietly bill you. feeedge.com adds the parts people skip

### F. Maker vs taker / limit vs market discussion (link when natural)
- some venues literally pay you to add liquidity. post a limit instead of a market order and you flip a cost into a rebate, if you can wait for the fill
- maker taker mix moves the ranking more than people expect. two traders on the same venue can pay completely different all in rates
- this is underrated. if you can be patient with fills the rebate side changes which venue is cheapest for you

### G. Pure value, no product (no link, build goodwill)
Use these to stay present without selling. Most of your daily replies are these.
- the volume tier thing gets me. sitting just under a breakpoint and a little more volume basically pays for itself
- withdrawal fees are the sneaky one. swing a lot by network and venue and they quietly outpace the trade fee if you self custody often
- agreed. fees are personal to how you trade, there is no single cheapest for everyone
- spread on a thin book can cost more than the fee. people stare at the price and forget the real cost of getting filled

## Part 3: the soft link, used right
- Default link: feeedge.com (free, plug your own numbers).
- Only when you have their actual profile do you hand them a prefilled result link,
  for example feeedge.com/r?v=500000&m=futures&mk=0.30&h=0.5 then let it unfurl.
- Never paste a link in your first reply of a thread. Add value, and if they reply
  or ask, then share.
- Max one link per conversation. If they bite, follow up human, no second link.

## Part 4: daily flow (about 20 minutes)
1. Run 2 or 3 of the search queries, open 10 to 15 fresh posts in tabs.
2. Reply value first to all of them, link on only the 4 or 5 that clearly fit.
3. Hit your running target list, reply to anyone who posted today.
4. Reply back to anyone who replied to you yesterday (this is where relationships form).
5. Log the day: rough reply count, any that landed, any link clicks.

## Part 5: staying safe
- Vary the wording every time. Do not paste the same draft twice in a day. These are
  starting points, change a few words so nothing reads templated.
- Space them out across the day, not 30 in one burst.
- No link in your first reply, ever. That single rule keeps the account clean.
