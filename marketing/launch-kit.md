## 2. Reddit

⚠️ **Reddit rules first:** most crypto subs restrict self-promotion. Read each sub's rules; post in a value-first way, disclose you built it, and engage in comments. Don't blast the same text everywhere on the same day — space them out and tailor each. Karma/age helps; if your account is new, comment genuinely for a few days first.

### r/CryptoCurrency (use the Daily Discussion or a "tool" flair if direct posts are restricted)
**Title:** I compared the real trading fees of 20 crypto exchanges — the gap is bigger than I expected
**Body:**
> I got annoyed that "which exchange is cheapest" has no clean answer — it depends on perps vs spot, your volume tier, and your maker/taker mix. So I pulled the published fees of 20 exchanges and built a calculator that ranks them by what you'd actually pay.
>
> A few things that surprised me:
> - At $1M/mo in perps, the cheapest vs priciest venue differs by ~$3,500/year for identical trades.
> - Bitfinex currently runs 0% maker/taker; MEXC is consistently among the cheapest perps.
> - Native-token discounts (BNB, OKB, KCS, etc.) range 10–25% and quietly change the ranking.
>
> Full write-up with the data: feeedge.com/report — and the calculator is feeedge.com (free).
>
> Disclosure: I built it. Genuinely after feedback on what fee factors I'm missing (funding, withdrawals, spreads are in there — what else?).

**Title:** Built a tool that ranks 20 exchanges by your *real* fee cost (perps + spot, funding included)

FeeEdge takes your volume + maker/taker mix and ranks all 20 venues by estimated monthly cost. Free: feeedge.com. Data + methodology are public. What fee factors would you weight differently?
