feeedge.com shows yours 👇

edie's fee tip 🤓 on a thin book the spread can cost you more than the fee. a "zero fee" venue with a wide spread is not actually cheap. judge total cost, not the headline rate.

feeedge.com 👇

edie's fee tip 🤓 zero fee still has costs: funding on perps, spreads, and withdrawals. free trading is a headline, not the whole bill. add it all up before you pick a venue.

feeedge.com does the math 👇

### Thread A: "where your fees leak"
1/ most active traders overpay on fees by 30 to 50 percent and never notice. it does not show up as one big charge, it leaks out in small ways. a short thread on where the money goes 🧵

3/ funding. on perps you pay or receive funding roughly every 8 hours. hold a leveraged position through a few windows and funding can cost more than the trade itself.

4/ withdrawals. moving coins off the exchange costs a fee that varies a lot by venue and network. if you self custody often, this adds up fast.

5/ the wrong venue. the cheapest exchange for a scalper at 2M a month is not the cheapest for someone swinging 50k. fees are personal to how you trade.
