# The hidden costs of crypto trading (it's not just the trading fee)

*Draft for a future /guides page. Target keywords: "crypto trading hidden fees", "funding rate cost", "crypto withdrawal fees", "true cost of trading crypto".*

When traders compare exchanges, they look at one number: the maker/taker trading fee. But that headline rate is only part of what you actually pay. Here are the costs that quietly add up — and why your "cheapest" exchange might not be the one with the lowest advertised fee.

## 1. Funding rates (perps only — often the biggest one)

If you trade perpetual futures, **funding** can dwarf your trading fees. Every 8 hours (hourly on some venues), longs and shorts exchange a payment to keep the perp price tethered to spot. Hold a leveraged position through several funding windows and you can pay more in funding than in trading fees — without ever placing another order.

Funding rates differ by exchange and by asset, and they swing with market sentiment. The cheapest venue to *hold* a position isn't always the cheapest to *trade* on.

## 2. Withdrawal fees (and the network you choose)

Moving crypto off an exchange costs a withdrawal fee, and it varies wildly — both by exchange and by **network**. The same USDT withdrawal might cost ~1 USDT on Tron (TRC-20) but several dollars on Ethereum (ERC-20). If you move funds often, picking the wrong chain is a recurring, avoidable tax.

## 3. The spread and slippage

The quoted fee assumes you trade at the mid price. In reality you cross the bid/ask spread, and on thin order books a market order walks the book — getting progressively worse fills (slippage). Less-liquid assets and smaller venues have wider spreads, so the *effective* cost of trading an altcoin can be much higher than the headline fee suggests.

## 4. Volume tiers you're not hitting

Most exchanges drop your rate as your 30-day volume climbs. If you're spread across three exchanges, you might be stuck at the worst tier on all of them — when consolidating onto one could unlock a cheaper tier and save more than switching venues entirely.

## 5. Native-token discounts you're not using

Several exchanges cut fees 10–25% if you hold or pay with their token (BNB, OKB, KCS, GT, BGB). If you trade there regularly and aren't using it, you're leaving an easy discount on the table.

## Add it all up

Real trading cost = trading fees **+ funding** (on perps) **+ withdrawal fees** **+ spread/slippage**, minus **volume-tier** and **token** discounts. The exchange with the lowest headline fee can easily lose to one with better funding, cheaper withdrawals, or a discount you'd actually use.
